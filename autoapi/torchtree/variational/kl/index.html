class torchtree.variational.kl.ELBO(id_: torchtree.typing.ID, q: torchtree.distributions.distributions.DistributionModel, p: torchtree.core.model.CallableModel, samples: torch.Size, entropy=False, score=False)[source]#

Bases: torchtree.core.model.CallableModel

Class representing the evidence lower bound (ELBO) objective.

The ELBO is defined as

\[\mathcal{L}(q) = \mathbb{E}_q[\log(p(z, x)] - \mathbb{E}_q[\log(q(z; \phi))]\]

Maximizing the ELBO wrt variational parameters \(\phi\) is equivalent to minimizing the exclusive Kullback-Leibler divergence from the posterior distribution \(p\) to the variational distribution \(q\) \(\text{KL}(q\|p) = \mathbb{E}_q[\log q(z; \phi)]-\mathbb{E}_q[\log p(z| x)]\).

The shape of samples is at most 2 dimensional.

  • 0 or 1 dimension N or [N]: standard ELBO.

  • 2 dimensions [N,K]: multi sample ELBO.

Parameters:

  • id (str or None) – ID of ELBO object.

  • q (DistributionModel) – variational distribution.

  • p (CallableModel) – joint distribution.

  • samples (torch.Size) – number of samples.

  • entropy (bool) – use entropy instead of Monte Carlo approximation for variational distribution

  • score (bool) – use score function instead of pathwise gradient estimator

class torchtree.variational.kl.KLpq(id_: torchtree.typing.ID, q: torchtree.distributions.distributions.DistributionModel, p: torchtree.core.model.CallableModel, samples: torch.Size)[source]#

Bases: torchtree.core.model.CallableModel

Calculate inclusive Kullback-Leibler divergence from q to p \(\text{KL}(p\|q)\) using self-normalized importance sampling gradient estimator.

The self-normalized importance sampling [1] estimate of \(\text{KL}(p \|q)\) using the instrument distribution \(q\) is

\[\begin{split}\widehat{KL}(p||q) & = \sum_{s=1}^S \log\left(\frac{p(\tilde{z}_s | x)} {q(\tilde{z}_s ; \phi)}\right) w_s , \quad \tilde{z}_s \sim q(z; \phi) \\ & \propto \sum_{s=1}^S \log\left(\frac{p(\tilde{z}_s)} {q(\tilde{z}_s;\phi)}\right) w_s\end{split}\]

where

\[w_s = \frac{p(\tilde{z}_s, D, \tau)}{ q(\tilde{z}_s; \phi)} / \sum_{i=1}^N \frac{p(\tilde{z}_i, D, \tau)}{q(\tilde{z}_i; \phi)}.\]
Parameters:

  • id (str or None) – ID of KLpq object.

  • q (DistributionModel) – variational distribution.

  • p (CallableModel) – joint distribution.

  • samples (torch.Size) – number of samples.

[1]

Kevin P Murphy. Machine learning: a probabilistic perspective. MIT press, 2012.

Class for minimizing inclusive Kullback-Leibler divergence from q to p \(\text{KL}(p\|q)\) using self-normalized importance sampling gradient estimator.

\[\nabla \widehat{\text{KL}}(p\|q) = -\sum_{s=1}^S w_s \nabla\log q(\tilde{z}_s ; \phi) , \quad \tilde{z}_s \sim q(z; \phi)\]

where

\[w_s = \frac{p(\tilde{z}_s, D, \tau)}{ q(\tilde{z}_s; \phi)} / \sum_{i=1}^N \frac{p(\tilde{z}_i, D, \tau)}{q(\tilde{z}_i; \phi)}.\]
Parameters:

  • id (str or None) – ID of object.

  • q (DistributionModel) – variational distribution.

  • p (CallableModel) – joint distribution.

  • samples (torch.Size) – number of samples.

class torchtree.variational.kl.SELBO(id_: torchtree.typing.ID, components: list[torchtree.distributions.distributions.DistributionModel], weights: torchtree.core.abstractparameter.AbstractParameter, p: torchtree.core.model.CallableModel, samples: torch.Size, entropy=False)[source]#

Bases: torchtree.core.model.CallableModel

Class representing the stratified evidence lower bound (SELBO) objective.

Maximizing the SELBO is equivalent to minimizing exclusive Kullback-Leibler divergence from p to q \(\text{KL}(q\|p)\) where \(q=\sum_i \alpha_i q_i\) [2].

The shape of samples is at most 2 dimensional.

  • 0 or 1 dimension N or [N]: standard ELBO.

  • 2 dimensions [N,K]: multi sample ELBO.

Parameters:

  • id (str or None) – ID of KLqp object.

  • components (DistributionModel) – list of distribution.

  • weights (AbstractParameter)

  • p (CallableModel) – joint distribution.

  • samples (torch.Size) – number of samples.

  • entropy (bool) – use entropy instead of Monte Carlo approximation for variational distribution

[2]

Warren Morningstar, Sharad Vikram, Cusuh Ham, Andrew Gallagher, and Joshua Dillon. Automatic differentiation variational inference with mixtures. In Arindam Banerjee and Kenji Fukumizu, editors, Proceedings of The 24th International Conference on Artificial Intelligence and Statistics, volume 130 of Proceedings of Machine Learning Research, 3250–3258. PMLR, 13–15 Apr 2021. URL: https://proceedings.mlr.press/v130/morningstar21b.html.
